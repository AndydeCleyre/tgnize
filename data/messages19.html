Our first experience at TeCGraf with a language designed in-house arose in a data-entry application. The engineers at PETROBRAS (the Brazilian oil company) needed to prepare input data files for simulators several times a day. This process was boring and error-prone because the simulation programs were legacy code that needed strictly formatted input files – typically bare columns of numbers, with no indication of what each number meant. Of course, each number had a specific meaning, which the engineers could grab at a glance, once they saw a diagram of the particular simulation. TeCGraf was asked by PETROBRAS to create several graphical front-ends for this kind of data entry.
21:06
